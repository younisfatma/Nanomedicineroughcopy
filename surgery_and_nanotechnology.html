Nanobots can be used for surgical purposes

The use of Nanotechnology in performing surgeries provides surgeons with a minimally invasive way to treat their patients. When lesser trama is inflicted upon the patient, recovery is faster, scarring is lessened and there are usually fewer complications in the aftermath of the operation. Nanotechnology for surgeons is extremely beneficial in many ways, tiny biosensors could be constructed which would shorten a patient's recovery period, save hospitals money, reduce infection rates, reduce the waiting lists for operations and doctors can treat more patients in the same period of time.

Surgery does not treat diseases nor does it heal. It only opens a way for the body to heal itself. A scalpel is millions of times bigger than a cell and even a precise cut is still very crude in terms of nano technological scale. In traditional open surgery, the surgeon cuts through healthy tissue in order to get to the internal organs that need to operate on. Recently, surgeons have been looking for ways for minimally invasive procedures. Nanoscale lasers are already being used in corneal surgery and have been used to investigate nerve regeneration in worms to provide insight into possible treatments for human neurological diseases
