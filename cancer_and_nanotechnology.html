Nanobots can now be used in fighting Cancer

Nanotechnology can now be used in treating Cancer. Scientist at Arizona State University, along with researchers from the National Center for Nanoscience and Technology (NCNST) of the Chinese Academy of Sciences, have successfully programmed nanorobots to shrink tumors by cutting off their blood supply. For this research the scientists took advantage of a well-known mouse tumor model, where human cancer cells are injected into the mouse to encourage aggressive tumor growth. Once the tumor was growing the nanobots were inserted into the mouse. These Nanobots were made from a flat, rectangular DNA origami sheet, 90 nanometers by 90 nanometers. Thrombin a blood clotting enzyme was attached to the surface of the Nanobot. The Thrombin blocks the tumor by clotting the blood to cut if the blood flow within the vessels that feed tumor growth. This causes the cancerous tissue to die. On average 4 thrombin molecules were attached the Nanobot. Next the Sheet is folded into a cylinder.

The Nanobots are injected into the mouse through an IV, and they travel through the bloodstream to the Tumors. A DNA aptamer is used to make sure the nanobots only attack cancer cells. The aptamer targets a protein called nucleation that's made in high amounts only on the tumor endothelial cells. Once the bots have found the tumor they are programed to find the heart of the Tumor and expose the thrombin. The nanobots quickly surround the tumor just hours after the injection. Then the blood supply is cut off killing of the tumor. These Scientists have shown that nanobots are safe and effective in shrinking tumors, although this is yet to be used in treating patients.
